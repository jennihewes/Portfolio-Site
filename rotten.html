Rotten.

A food management app that helps people keep track of what groceries they have to avoid food waste.

My Role

  • UX/UI Designer

Tools Used

  • Figma (Wireframes, Prototyping)
  • UsabilityHub (Preference Tests)
  • Google Forms (User Surveys)
Link to Prototype

The Problem

Everyday food is thrown out because it has expired. People buy items they already have without realizing it. In homes and apartments with many people, it is especially difficult to manage food. People need a way to keep track of food that they have so it is not wasted.

“When I’m at the grocery store, I can’t remember what I have at home.”

“I share certain foods with my room mate. We always buy the same items and end up with two of everything.”

“I end up throwing out meat and vegetables because I lose track of expiration dates.”

The User

“We have three bottles of ketchup in our apartment. I need a way to communicate with my room mates about what food we have.”

The Urban Roomate

Ages 20-30

Behaviors

  • Goes to the grocery store once a week
  • Cooks 3-4 dinners a week for 1 person. Goes out to eat frequently
  • Does not make a grocery list before going to the grocery store
  • Does not enjoy cooking and is always looking for new simple recipes

Frustrations

  • Buying shared groceries like spices and condiments that are already in the apartment
  • Having to think of what to cook after a long day

“I have to cook large dinners for my hungry family. It takes a lot of planning to make sure meals get cooked and food doesn’t go to waste.”

The Suburban Organizer

Ages 30-40

Behaviors

  • Goes to the grocery store twice a week
  • Cooks 5-6 dinners a week for 5 people. Does not go out to eat to save money
  • Makes a grocery list before going to the grocery store
  • s a deal shopper - will stock up on an item if it is on sale

Frustrations

  • When plans change and food doesn’t get cooked and expires
  • Has used food management apps in the past, but finds it tedious to keep lists updated

Brainstorm

Issue

People need a way to keep track of expiration dates and what food they have to avoid wasting food.

Idea

Design a food list that is organized by category and stores expiration dates.

Issue

Roomates need a way to manage the food they share.

Idea

Create a feature for room mates to share lists.

Issue

Updating grocery apps can be manual and time consuming.

Idea

Have a scan receipt feature to automatically update what items are at home.

Usability Study

6 users participated in a study to identity pain points in the prototype. They were asked to complete these 6 tasks.

Usability Study Results

The biggest issue was with task 4 that asked users to create a space to keep track of their spices. 100% of participants could not complete this task.

In my orignial design, I had the idea for there to be different types of food storage. For example, users could organize where to store food such as spice rack, pantry, or freezer.

During the usability study I learned that the different types of storage confused users. When asked to create a list for spices, participants went to the list of all food and tried to create a list from there.

This lead me to remove the different food storage types. It was clear that it was not intutive and caused confusion for the user.

The Final Design

Home Page

The home page highlights expiration dates to help users avoid food waste. Users can also mark items as running low and add them to their shopping lists.

My Food

My Food is where users keep track of all the food they have at home. My Food is organized by food category and users can mark when food is running low. Users can scan their receipt to automatically update My Food.

Recipes

Users can select specific ingredients and search for recipes that include those ingredients. They will also be shown recipes that are missing a few ingredients.

Friends

Users can share food lists with room mates and friends. This will help people living together to avoid buying duplicate shared items.
