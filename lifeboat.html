Lifeboat

A marine weather app designed for boaters to check weather conditions.

Lifeboat is a marine weather app designed for boaters to check weather conditions.

My Role

  • UX/UI Designer

Tools Used

  • Adobe XD (Wireframes, Prototyping)
  • Balsamiq (Wireframes)
  • Optimal Workshop (Card Sorting)
  • UsabilityHub (Preference Tests)
  • Google Forms (User Surveys)

The Problem

Boating enthusiasts need specific weather metrics that aren't found on every weather app. To determine what ocean conditions are like, they need to know swell height, tide times, wind direction, wind speed, and visibility. Current weather apps have one but not all the weather metrics boaters want. Boaters end up checking 2-4 sources to get all the information they need.

Problem Statement

Boaters need a simple way to determine if weather conditions are safe on the ocean. We will know this to be true when users are checking the app when planning a boat excursion.

User Stories

  • As a boater, I want to see metrics like swell size and tides so I can determine when and where to go out on the boat.
  • As a boater, I want a recommendation of ocean conditions.
  • As a boater, I want to save a favorite location so I don’t have to re-type it each time.

The User

The Leisurely Retiree

Ages 60+

“I am used to planning boat trips using old fashioned methods. I am open to try new apps if they aren’t too confusing for me.”

Behaviors

  • Has more time to take long trips. Enjoys being spontaneous
  • Checks the weather section while reading the newspaper and on his phone
  • Desktop computer is his primary device
  • Checks the weather at his starting point and destination
  • Checks the weather 3 days in advance

Frustrations

  • Finds crowded interfaces to be confusing
  • Wants a recommendation so he doesn't have to comb through weather data

The Busy Boater

Ages 25-34

“I like to plan ahead to pick the best day for boating. I check 3 apps to understand what the weather conditions will be.”

Behaviors

  • Likes to plan ahead. Has less free time so wants his days on the ocean to be worth it
  • Checks 3 different sources to find all the weather data he needs
  • Checks the weather everyday in 1-2 minute sessions
  • Checks the weather 7 days in advance

Frustrations

  • Having to check multiple weather sources to get all the information he wants
  • Each source has a different format. One has visualizations, one has metrics, and one has a written description

Brainstorm

Issue

Boaters are frustrated with having to check multiple weather sources to get all the information they need.

Brainstorm

Design a home page that takes a lot of information and makes it easy to read. Ensure all information boaters need is included.

Solution

All pertinent information on the home page: temperature, precipitation, wind, swell height, visibility, tide times, and sun times.

Issue

Boaters want a recommendation rather than comb through weather data.

Brainstorm

Design a visual that gives a simple recommendation of how safe the ocean is. This way boaters can know ocean conditions at a glance.

Solution

A safety meter that analyzes weather data and gives a safety rating.

Issue

Avid boaters have favorite spots they go to. They need an easy way to check the weather at their destinations.

Brainstorm

Create a way for boaters to check the weather at 2 spots: their starting point and destination.

Solution

A trips section where users can check the weather at destinations and save favorite destinations.

The Design

The Site Map

Lifeboat is organized into 4 sections that users can switch between. Users interact with the app daily in short sessions and it is important that they can easily find information.

The site map was revised after performing a card sorting exercise to understand how people categorized content. A key take away from card sorting was that users don’t categorize tide and sunset times with other weather data.

Usability Study

6 users participated in a study to identify pain points in the prototype. These issues were found and revisions were made in the following iteration.

The Design

Chakra Petch was chosen as the primary font because it is nautical and scientific. I was inspired to use orange by the color of lifeboats.

The orange filled icons accent blue and white that are used on the background and text.

The Final Product

Home Page

The main feature of the home page is the safety meter. A key takeaway from my initial user interviews, was that boaters want a summary of weather conditions so they don't have to comb through data. Ocean specific metrics are also available at a glance on the home page.

Weather

Boaters check the weather everyday leading up to a boating excursion. The weather section gives users an hourly view of each weather metric. In the usability study, I tested a different hourly visual that looked like a clock. Feedback was mixed and I replaced it with a graph that is more digestible.

Trips

Boaters check the weather in 2 places - their starting point and destination. The trips section allows users to see the weather at both places at once. Users can save favorite destinations.

Settings

Boaters can save details about their boat and marina under settings. The starting location will default to the My Marina location. The safety meter's algorithm will take into consideration the type of boat when determining ocean safety.
